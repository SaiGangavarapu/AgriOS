# BRD 23 — Water Sampling and Quality

## 1. Objective

Assess whether a water source is suitable for irrigation and identify constraints that affect crop selection, soil management, and long-term productivity.

## 2. Sampling contexts

- new source
- seasonal change
- suspected salinity
- borewell-depth change
- contamination event
- repeated crop stress
- certification requirement
- shared-source dispute

## 3. Water-test profile

Potential parameters:

- pH
- electrical conductivity
- total dissolved solids
- sodium
- calcium
- magnesium
- bicarbonate
- chloride
- nitrate
- microbial contamination where relevant
- other locally required contaminants

## 4. Source linkage

A test may apply to:

- one source
- multiple fields
- one season
- one pumping depth
- one storage tank

The linkage and validity period must be explicit.

## 5. Interpretation

The system should assess:

- salinity risk
- sodicity risk
- infiltration risk
- toxicity risk
- crop sensitivity
- soil interaction
- irrigation-method suitability

## 6. Critical alerts

Critical results may trigger:

- expert review
- crop restriction
- blending recommendation
- leaching or drainage review
- alternative source recommendation
- contamination escalation

## 7. Business requirements

- BR-082: The platform shall register water samples against sources.
- BR-083: Water results shall retain method, unit, and validity.
- BR-084: Water constraints shall influence crop and irrigation plans.
- BR-085: Critical quality results shall trigger escalation.
- BR-086: Historical water-quality trends shall be retained.
