# BRD 06 — Scope and Boundaries

## 1. Initial product scope

### Farmer and identity

- farmer onboarding
- household and organization relationships
- role-based access
- consent

### Farm and field

- farm registration
- field registration
- GPS boundary
- land tenure
- water sources
- assets

### Agronomy

- soil and water tests
- crop and variety knowledge
- crop planning
- crop-cycle management
- farm operations
- nutrient and irrigation plans
- observation and scouting

### Weather

- forecast integration
- warnings
- weather-aware advisories
- forecast confidence

### IoT

- device registry
- installation
- telemetry
- health
- calibration
- alerts
- limited safe automation

### Expert workflows

- review queue
- consultation
- recommendation approval
- audit

### Market and economics

- input cost
- operational cost
- expected and actual yield
- market-price reference
- profitability summary

### Experience

- mobile-first PWA or app
- multilingual UI
- voice input and playback
- offline-first operation
- notifications

## 2. Deferred scope

- national rollout
- autonomous pesticide prescription
- autonomous credit or insurance decisions
- full drone operations
- variable-rate machinery control
- advanced ML recommendations
- broad marketplace fulfilment
- full accounting ERP
- complete livestock integration

## 3. Explicit exclusions

- guarantee of yield
- guarantee of market price
- replacement of licensed experts
- replacement of official warnings
- unreviewed medical or veterinary advice
- use of farmer data without consent
- treating low-cost sensors as unquestioned laboratory evidence

## 4. Geographic scope

The architecture is extensible, but the initial business design is India-first and pilot-driven.

## 5. Pilot boundary

The pilot should limit:

- geography
- crops
- user groups
- devices
- integrations
- supported languages
- recommendation categories

This enables field validation before expansion.
