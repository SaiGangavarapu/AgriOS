# BRD 34 — Nutrient Management Planning

## 1. Objective

Create field-specific nutrient plans that meet crop demand while protecting soil health, farmer finances, water quality, and long-term productivity.

## 2. Supported pathways

- conventional fertilizer-based plan
- integrated nutrient-management plan
- certified-organic plan
- natural or low-external-input plan
- transition plan between farming systems

## 3. Plan inputs

- crop and variety
- field and management zone
- crop stage
- soil-test results
- water quality
- expected yield range
- previous crop
- residue management
- manure and compost availability
- biofertilizer availability
- irrigation method
- weather forecast
- farmer budget
- certification restrictions

## 4. Nutrient budget

For each nutrient, the plan should consider:

- estimated soil supply
- nutrient credits
- organic-source contribution
- mineral fertilizer contribution
- irrigation-water contribution
- biological fixation
- expected crop removal
- expected losses
- residual effect

## 5. Plan outputs

- nutrient requirement by nutrient
- source recommendations
- product quantities
- basal and split schedule
- placement method
- fertigation schedule where applicable
- weather restrictions
- crop-stage restrictions
- expected cost
- uncertainty
- monitoring and reassessment points

## 6. Plan comparison

For each supported pathway, display:

- nutrient adequacy
- purchase cost
- labour
- number of applications
- expected release pattern
- soil-health contribution
- environmental risk
- transition risk
- evidence confidence

## 7. Lifecycle

`Draft → Generated → Reviewed → Approved → Active → Revised → Completed`

## 8. Business requirements

- BR-137: The platform shall support multiple nutrient-management pathways.
- BR-138: Nutrient plans shall use current field, crop, soil, water, and stage context.
- BR-139: Plans shall expose nutrient credits and assumptions.
- BR-140: Plans shall distinguish nutrient requirement from commercial-product quantity.
- BR-141: Plan revisions shall preserve prior versions and rationale.
- BR-142: Low-confidence nutrient plans shall require expert review.
