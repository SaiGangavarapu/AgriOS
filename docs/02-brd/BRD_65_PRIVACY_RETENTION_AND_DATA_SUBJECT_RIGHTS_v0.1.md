# BRD 65 — Privacy, Retention, and Data-Subject Rights

## 1. Objective

Protect farmer and participant data throughout collection, use, sharing, retention, correction, and deletion.

## 2. Privacy principles

- purpose limitation
- data minimization
- informed consent
- access control
- accuracy
- retention limitation
- transparency
- accountability
- security
- controlled sharing

## 3. Rights workflows

Where applicable, users should be able to request:

- access
- correction
- consent review
- consent withdrawal
- export
- deletion
- restriction
- complaint
- explanation of automated support

## 4. Retention classes

- identity
- farm and field
- agronomy history
- telemetry
- images
- voice
- audit
- financial evidence
- support records
- research datasets

Retention shall be policy-driven and may differ by legal, safety, research, or contractual need.

## 5. Deletion and anonymization

Deletion may require:

- physical deletion
- logical deletion
- irreversible anonymization
- restricted retention for legal evidence
- downstream notification

## 6. Data sharing

Every share should record:

- purpose
- recipient
- data scope
- lawful or consent basis
- expiry
- export or access method
- revocation status

## 7. Business requirements

- BR-293: The platform shall support data-access, correction, export, and deletion workflows where applicable.
- BR-294: Retention shall be configurable by data class and purpose.
- BR-295: Deletion shall account for legal, safety, and audit obligations.
- BR-296: Data sharing shall retain recipient, purpose, scope, and expiry.
- BR-297: Research use shall require anonymization or separate consent as applicable.
