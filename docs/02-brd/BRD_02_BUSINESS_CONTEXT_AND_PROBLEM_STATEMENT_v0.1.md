# BRD 02 — Business Context and Problem Statement

## 1. Agricultural operating context

Farmers operate under uncertainty created by:

- variable rainfall
- changing climate patterns
- soil variability
- fragmented land
- changing input prices
- limited credit
- labour availability
- pest and disease outbreaks
- market volatility
- unreliable connectivity
- inconsistent advisory access

## 2. Current-state challenges

### Fragmented records

Farm data may be spread across notebooks, invoices, messages, laboratory reports, and memory.

### Generic advice

Recommendations may be provided at district or crop level without considering the specific field, soil, water, crop stage, or farmer budget.

### Commercial bias

Input sellers may influence recommendations, creating conflicts of interest.

### Weak timing

Correct advice delivered too late may have little value.

### Limited expert reach

Agronomists and specialists cannot personally inspect every field.

### Weak evidence traceability

Farmers and institutions may not know which source, rule, or expert produced an advisory.

### Weather uncertainty

Forecasts may disagree, change, or be misunderstood as guarantees.

### Sensor quality risk

Low-cost devices can create misleading precision when calibration and placement are poor.

### Data-sharing distrust

Farmers may fear that data will be used against them or shared without consent.

## 3. Problem statement

Farmers lack a unified, field-specific, trustworthy, explainable, and accessible system for planning and operating crop production under agronomic, weather, financial, and market uncertainty.

## 4. Root causes

- absence of a shared field digital twin
- unstructured local knowledge
- disconnected government and private data sources
- limited interoperability
- weak offline support
- low local-language usability
- incomplete consent and governance
- technology-first rather than farmer-first deployments

## 5. Desired future state

A farmer should be able to open one application and understand:

- what must be done today
- why it must be done
- when it should be completed
- how much input or water is required
- what risk exists if it is delayed
- how confident the recommendation is
- what evidence or expert supports it
