# BRD 26 — Crop Planning

## 1. Objective

Create an actionable and financially understandable crop plan before sowing.

## 2. Crop-plan contents

- field
- season
- crop
- variety
- sowing window
- planned area
- farming system
- seed requirement
- nutrient approach
- irrigation approach
- labour estimate
- machinery estimate
- expected input cost
- expected yield range
- price scenarios
- gross-margin range
- risks
- contingency plan

## 3. Plan comparison

Farmers should compare:

- conventional plan
- integrated plan
- organic plan
- natural or low-external-input plan

where sufficient evidence exists.

## 4. Approval

Plan states:

`Draft → Generated → Farmer Reviewed → Expert Review Required/Not Required → Approved → Activated`

## 5. Revision

A plan may be revised due to:

- delayed rain
- seed unavailability
- water shortage
- price change
- field condition
- labour shortage
- pest or disease risk

## 6. Business requirements

- BR-097: The platform shall generate field-specific crop plans.
- BR-098: Plans shall include cost, yield, risk, and contingency information.
- BR-099: Plans shall support farming-system comparison.
- BR-100: Plan approval shall be explicit.
- BR-101: Revisions shall preserve history and rationale.
