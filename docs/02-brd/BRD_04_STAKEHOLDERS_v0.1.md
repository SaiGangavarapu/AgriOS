# BRD 04 — Stakeholders

## 1. Primary stakeholders

### Farmers

Need practical, timely, understandable, affordable guidance.

### Farm workers and family members

Need clear assigned tasks and simple recording flows.

### Field officers

Need farm portfolios, visit planning, issue escalation, and offline data capture.

### Agronomists

Need field context, observations, evidence, review queues, and advisory tools.

### Soil and water laboratories

Need sample traceability, result exchange, and quality metadata.

### KVKs and agricultural universities

Need validated knowledge publication, expert review, and programme reporting.

## 2. Commercial stakeholders

### Input suppliers

May provide catalogue and availability data but must not control agronomic recommendations.

### Equipment and IoT providers

Need device registration, installation, maintenance, and interoperability.

### Buyers and aggregators

Need harvest availability, quality, lot traceability, and farmer consent.

### Warehouses and logistics providers

Need storage, movement, and quality information.

## 3. Financial and institutional stakeholders

### Insurers

Need consented, trustworthy evidence for enrolment, risk assessment, and claims.

### Banks and lenders

Need consented records and must not treat unverified app data as unquestioned truth.

### Government agencies

Need programme delivery, advisory reach, and aggregated reporting under defined governance.

### NGOs and development programmes

Need field monitoring, intervention tracking, and outcome measurement.

### Researchers

Need anonymized, governed, quality-scored datasets.

## 4. Internal stakeholders

- product management
- business analysis
- engineering
- security
- privacy
- operations
- support
- data science
- quality assurance
- legal and compliance
- domain-governance board

## 5. Stakeholder conflicts

Potential conflicts include:

- farmer benefit versus commercial sales
- privacy versus institutional data needs
- speed versus expert review
- automation versus safety
- local practices versus standardized guidance
- open data versus proprietary licensing

These conflicts must be explicitly governed rather than hidden.
