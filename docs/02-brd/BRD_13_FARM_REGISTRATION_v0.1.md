# BRD 13 — Farm Registration

## 1. Objective

Create a durable farm-level record that groups fields, water sources, infrastructure, assets, operators, and historical operations.

## 2. Farm definition

A farm is an operational agricultural unit managed by one or more farmers. It may include owned, leased, shared, or institution-managed land.

## 3. Farm profile

- farm name
- primary operator
- participating farmers
- village and location
- operational area
- production systems
- irrigation profile
- storage availability
- electricity or energy source
- livestock linkage
- machinery linkage
- certification status
- organization affiliation

## 4. Farm types

- individual family farm
- tenant-operated farm
- jointly operated farm
- FPO-managed demonstration farm
- institutional farm
- leased farm cluster
- community farm

## 5. Farm lifecycle

`Draft → Registered → Verified → Active → Inactive → Archived`

## 6. Verification

Farm verification may include:

- field-officer visit
- boundary confirmation
- organization confirmation
- land document reference
- farmer declaration
- geotagged evidence

The verification method and confidence must be visible.

## 7. Business requirements

- BR-031: The platform shall support multiple farms per farmer.
- BR-032: A farm shall support multiple participating farmers and roles.
- BR-033: Farm verification shall be method-specific and auditable.
- BR-034: Farm status changes shall not delete historical crop cycles.
- BR-035: The platform shall support farm-level infrastructure and asset references.
