# BRD 44 — Expert Review and Consultation

## 1. Objective

Route high-risk, low-confidence, unusual, or disputed cases to qualified experts.

## 2. Expert roles

- agronomist
- soil scientist
- irrigation specialist
- agrometeorologist
- entomologist
- plant pathologist
- certification specialist
- IoT technician

## 3. Review triggers

- low confidence
- critical soil or water result
- conflicting recommendations
- suspected severe disease
- repeated crop failure
- restricted input
- automation request
- farmer disagreement
- unusual sensor pattern

## 4. Review case

- farmer and field
- crop cycle
- issue category
- urgency
- observations
- photos
- soil and water results
- weather
- telemetry
- system recommendation
- evidence
- farmer question

## 5. Review lifecycle

`Created → Triaged → Assigned → In Review → Information Requested → Recommendation Issued → Farmer Communicated → Closed`

## 6. Service levels

Configurable by:

- severity
- crop stage
- risk type
- expert specialty
- programme
- geography

## 7. Consultation modes

- asynchronous review
- phone consultation
- video consultation
- field visit
- group advisory
- institutional escalation

## 8. Business requirements

- BR-188: The platform shall route cases by specialty, severity, and geography.
- BR-189: Expert reviews shall retain evidence and rationale.
- BR-190: Experts shall be able to request additional information.
- BR-191: Review service levels and overdue escalation shall be configurable.
- BR-192: Expert decisions shall supersede system advice only through an auditable workflow.
