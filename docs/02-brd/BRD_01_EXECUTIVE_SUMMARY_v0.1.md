# BRD 01 — Executive Summary

## 1. Overview

AgriOS is an agriculture operating platform designed to help farmers and agricultural institutions make field-specific, evidence-based decisions across the farming lifecycle.

The platform will integrate:

- farmer, farm, and field records
- soil and water intelligence
- crop and variety planning
- nutrient and irrigation management
- weather and monsoon intelligence
- IoT and edge monitoring
- pest and disease observations
- expert review
- market and profitability support
- local-language AI assistance
- offline-first mobile workflows
- consent-based data sharing

## 2. Business problem

Agricultural decisions are often fragmented across paper records, verbal advice, commercial recommendations, public advisories, laboratory reports, and personal experience.

This fragmentation creates:

- inconsistent recommendations
- avoidable input expenditure
- poor timing of farm operations
- weak traceability
- weather-related losses
- inefficient water use
- limited access to experts
- difficulty comparing farming strategies
- limited trust for insurers, lenders, buyers, and institutions

## 3. Business proposition

AgriOS will create a field-level digital operating model that continuously combines:

- field context
- crop stage
- soil and water data
- weather observations and forecasts
- farmer constraints
- agronomy rules
- expert review
- economic context

The system will produce explainable tasks, advisories, warnings, and plans rather than generic information.

## 4. Primary beneficiaries

- small and marginal farmers
- progressive farmers
- tenant farmers
- farmer producer organizations
- field officers
- agronomists
- KVKs and universities
- laboratories
- input and equipment providers
- buyers and aggregators
- insurers and financial institutions
- government and development agencies

## 5. Strategic value

AgriOS is intended to become the shared agricultural foundation for:

- farmer decision support
- livestock management
- milk collection and dairy operations
- farm-fresh commerce
- meat and egg value chains
- configurable seller operations
- food processing
- finance and insurance integrations
- government and research programmes

## 6. Delivery approach

The programme will begin with documentation, domain modelling, and a controlled pilot. Machine learning and automation will be introduced only after validated field data and governance controls exist.
