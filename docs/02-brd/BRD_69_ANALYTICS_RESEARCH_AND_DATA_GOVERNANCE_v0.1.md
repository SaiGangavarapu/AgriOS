# BRD 69 — Analytics, Research, and Data Governance

## 1. Objective

Enable trustworthy operational analytics and governed research without compromising farmer rights or overstating data quality.

## 2. Analytics categories

- adoption
- crop planning
- advisory delivery
- operations
- soil and water
- weather impact
- device performance
- yield and economics
- market
- programme outcomes
- support quality

## 3. Data quality dimensions

- completeness
- validity
- timeliness
- consistency
- provenance
- verification
- representativeness
- bias
- uncertainty

## 4. Research access

Research workflows shall support:

- proposal
- purpose review
- ethics or governance review
- data minimization
- anonymization
- approval
- time-bound access
- output review
- closure

## 5. Anonymization

The platform should reduce re-identification risk through:

- removal of direct identifiers
- geographic aggregation
- date generalization
- thresholded reporting
- suppression
- controlled linkage
- access restrictions

## 6. Model training

Training datasets shall record:

- source
- consent or legal basis
- cohort
- geography
- period
- labels
- quality
- exclusions
- version
- bias assessment

## 7. Business requirements

- BR-313: Analytics shall expose data-quality and provenance limitations.
- BR-314: Research access shall require approved purpose and time-bound scope.
- BR-315: Anonymized outputs shall apply re-identification controls.
- BR-316: Model-training datasets shall be versioned and governed.
- BR-317: Operational dashboards shall not expose unauthorized farmer-level data.
