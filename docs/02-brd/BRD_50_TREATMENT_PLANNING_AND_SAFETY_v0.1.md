# BRD 50 — Treatment Planning and Safety

## 1. Objective

Support integrated pest and disease management using preventive, cultural, mechanical, biological, and chemical options under safety and legal controls.

## 2. Treatment hierarchy

Where applicable, present:

1. prevention
2. monitoring
3. cultural control
4. mechanical control
5. biological control
6. approved chemical control
7. expert or regulatory escalation

## 3. Treatment-plan inputs

- confirmed or suspected issue
- confidence
- crop
- stage
- field
- severity and incidence
- weather
- prior treatment
- resistance-management history
- farming system
- product approval
- harvest interval
- operator capability

## 4. Chemical-control safeguards

- approved crop and target
- legal registration
- dose and unit
- maximum applications
- minimum interval
- pre-harvest interval
- re-entry interval
- weather suitability
- PPE
- water-body and pollinator precautions
- resistance group rotation
- storage and disposal

## 5. Treatment lifecycle

`Draft → Review Required/Not Required → Approved → Scheduled → Applied → Effectiveness Review → Closed`

## 6. Effectiveness review

Record:

- follow-up date
- symptom progression
- pest count
- crop response
- adverse effect
- re-treatment decision
- expert note

## 7. Business requirements

- BR-218: The platform shall support integrated treatment options.
- BR-219: Chemical treatment shall require legal, crop, stage, dose, weather, and safety validation.
- BR-220: Pre-harvest and re-entry intervals shall be enforced where applicable.
- BR-221: Treatment effectiveness and adverse effects shall be recorded.
- BR-222: Treatment history shall support resistance-management checks.
