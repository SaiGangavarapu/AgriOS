# BRD 15 — Land Tenure and Cultivation Rights

## 1. Objective

Represent who owns, controls, cultivates, and benefits from agricultural land without excluding tenant or informal cultivators.

## 2. Tenure types

- owned
- leased
- sharecropped
- family-owned and jointly cultivated
- community-managed
- institution-managed
- informal cultivation
- disputed
- unknown

## 3. Tenure record

- field
- tenure type
- owner or declaring party
- cultivating party
- start date
- end date
- agreement reference
- verification status
- evidence type
- share arrangement where relevant
- restrictions

## 4. Privacy rule

Land documents and identity evidence must not be broadly visible. Only derived verification status should be shown to users without a legitimate need.

## 5. Tenant-farmer support

The platform shall permit:

- seasonal crop planning
- farm-operation recording
- crop-cycle ownership
- input and cost tracking
- consented evidence generation

without falsely asserting legal land ownership.

## 6. Conflict handling

Potential conflicts:

- multiple active cultivators
- overlapping lease dates
- owner objection
- disputed boundary
- expired agreement
- unclear share arrangement

The platform should flag, restrict sensitive sharing, and preserve declarations and evidence.

## 7. Business requirements

- BR-041: The platform shall distinguish ownership from cultivation.
- BR-042: The platform shall support tenant and sharecropping arrangements.
- BR-043: The platform shall preserve tenure history.
- BR-044: The platform shall support confidential tenure evidence.
- BR-045: The platform shall not infer legal ownership from operational records.
