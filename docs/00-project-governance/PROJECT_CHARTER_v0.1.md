# AgriOS Project Charter

## 1. Project name

AgriOS — Agriculture Operating System

## 2. Purpose

AgriOS will provide an evidence-based digital platform that helps farmers make better decisions across the complete farming lifecycle, while enabling experts and institutions to review, validate, and improve those decisions.

## 3. Problem statement

Farmers often make crop, seed, fertilizer, irrigation, pest, and harvest decisions using fragmented information. Recommendations may not account for the specific field, soil test, water availability, crop stage, weather forecast, farmer budget, or local market conditions.

The platform will address this by creating a field-level digital decision system rather than a generic information portal.

## 4. Primary objectives

- Improve field-level decision quality
- Reduce avoidable input waste
- Improve soil and water stewardship
- Support natural, organic, integrated, and conventional farming pathways
- Improve weather-informed farm planning
- Enable expert validation and traceability
- Support rural connectivity constraints
- Provide local-language and voice-first experiences
- Build a shared foundation for agriculture, livestock, dairy, food processing, and farm commerce products

## 5. Initial users

- Farmers
- Farm workers
- Field officers
- Agronomists
- Soil scientists
- Plant pathologists
- Entomologists
- Laboratories
- Krishi Vigyan Kendras
- Agricultural universities
- Farmer producer organizations
- Buyers and aggregators
- Insurers and financial institutions
- Government and research partners

## 6. Initial scope

- Farmer, farm, and field registry
- Soil and water assessment
- Crop and seed suitability
- Crop-cycle planning
- Nutrient management
- Irrigation planning
- Weather advisories
- IoT device management
- Pest and disease observation
- Task calendar
- Expert review
- Offline mobile support
- Consent and traceability

## 7. Out of scope for the first release

- Fully autonomous pesticide prescription
- Fully autonomous financial or insurance approval
- National-scale rollout
- Drone fleet management
- Variable-rate application hardware control
- Machine-learning recommendations without validated field data

## 8. Guiding principle

The platform must explain what to do, when to do it, how much to use, why the recommendation exists, the confidence level, and the evidence or expert source behind it.
