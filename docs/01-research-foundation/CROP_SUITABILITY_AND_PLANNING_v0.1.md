# Crop Suitability and Planning

## 1. Planning objective

Create ranked crop plans for a specific field and season rather than issue generic district-level recommendations.

## 2. Input dimensions

### Field

- area
- soil
- slope
- drainage
- erosion risk
- field shape
- access

### Water

- source
- reliability
- quality
- expected quantity
- irrigation method
- energy cost

### Climate

- seasonal rainfall outlook
- onset uncertainty
- dry-spell risk
- temperature
- heat and cold extremes
- humidity
- wind
- growing-period length

### Farm capability

- capital
- credit
- labour
- machinery
- knowledge
- storage
- transport

### Market

- expected price range
- local demand
- buyer requirement
- grading
- perishability
- storage
- transport cost

### Rotation and ecosystem

- previous crop
- pest carry-over
- disease carry-over
- nutrient effect
- residue
- livestock or fodder need
- biodiversity objective

## 3. Hard constraints

Examples:

- crop cannot mature within the expected growing period
- irrigation requirement exceeds reliable supply
- soil salinity exceeds crop tolerance
- drainage is unsuitable
- seed is unavailable
- planting date is outside validated contingency guidance

## 4. Scored criteria

- yield potential
- margin range
- climate resilience
- water-use fit
- soil fit
- farmer familiarity
- market stability
- rotation value
- food and fodder value

## 5. Output

For each candidate crop:

- suitability score
- rejected constraints
- recommended varieties
- sowing window
- expected duration
- water demand
- investment range
- yield range
- price scenarios
- risk factors
- confidence
- contingency crop

## 6. Scenario planning

At minimum:

- normal rainfall
- delayed onset
- early-season dry spell
- limited irrigation
- heavy-rain period
- price downside

## 7. Crop plan approval

The farmer may choose a lower-ranked crop for valid reasons. The system should record the decision and rationale without blocking the farmer unless there is a safety or legal constraint.
