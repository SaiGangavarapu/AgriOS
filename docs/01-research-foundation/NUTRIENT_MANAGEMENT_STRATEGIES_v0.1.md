# Nutrient Management Strategies

## 1. Objective

Meet crop nutrient demand while protecting soil, water, farmer finances, and long-term productivity.

## 2. Nutrient budget

For each nutrient:

`Opening supply + additions - crop removal - estimated losses = closing balance`

Sources may include:

- soil supply
- mineral fertilizer
- manure
- compost
- crop residue
- green manure
- biological fixation
- irrigation water
- previous crop credits

## 3. The 4R concept

Plans should consider:

- right source
- right rate
- right time
- right place

## 4. Conventional plan

Requirements:

- current representative soil test
- crop and stage
- target-yield range
- nutrient credits
- source analysis
- split schedule
- weather constraints
- application method
- maximum and minimum safeguards

## 5. Integrated nutrient management

Combine:

- mineral sources
- organic sources
- crop residues
- legumes
- green manure
- biofertilizers
- rotations
- site-specific micronutrient correction

The plan must account for variable nutrient concentration and release from organic materials.

## 6. Organic plan

The platform must maintain:

- permitted-input status
- certification scheme
- supplier and lot
- application log
- conversion period
- contamination controls
- buffer requirements

## 7. Natural or low-external-input plan

The plan should quantify:

- biomass availability
- manure availability
- compost maturity
- legume contribution
- expected nutrient release
- nutrient export in harvest
- transition risk
- monitoring schedule

The system must not imply that all farm-made inputs have predictable nutrient content.

## 8. Micronutrients

Before recommending:

- confirm deficiency evidence
- check soil and tissue data
- review pH and interactions
- verify crop sensitivity
- prevent repeated accumulation
- follow current local guidance

## 9. Rain and irrigation rules

Potential lockouts:

- heavy rain forecast
- saturated field
- strong runoff risk
- high wind for foliar application
- irrigation equipment failure

## 10. Product conversion

The calculation engine must distinguish nutrient requirement from commercial product quantity and account for product nutrient concentration.

## 11. Plan comparison

Show:

- nutrient adequacy
- purchase cost
- labour
- application count
- timing flexibility
- expected release
- uncertainty
- soil-health contribution
- environmental risk
