# Irrigation and Water Management

## 1. Objective

Apply sufficient water at the correct time while avoiding stress, waterlogging, salinity, energy waste, and nutrient loss.

## 2. Water sources

- rainfall
- canal
- tank
- pond
- borewell
- open well
- harvested water
- treated water where legally and agronomically acceptable

## 3. Water-source profile

Capture:

- seasonal availability
- flow or yield
- storage
- pumping capacity
- energy source
- cost
- quality
- restrictions
- sharing arrangements

## 4. Soil-water concepts

- saturation
- field capacity
- permanent wilting point
- available water
- root-zone depletion
- infiltration
- runoff
- deep percolation

## 5. Crop water demand

Depends on:

- crop
- stage
- canopy
- weather
- soil
- root depth
- management
- irrigation efficiency

## 6. Scheduling inputs

- recent rainfall
- forecast rainfall
- soil moisture
- crop stage
- evapotranspiration
- soil water-holding capacity
- irrigation method
- field observations

## 7. Irrigation methods

- surface
- furrow
- basin
- sprinkler
- drip
- micro-sprinkler
- manual localized application

Each method requires configurable efficiency and operating constraints.

## 8. Sensor placement

Soil-moisture sensors should represent:

- management zone
- soil type
- root depth
- emitter or row geometry
- wetting pattern

One sensor should not automatically represent a heterogeneous field.

## 9. Water-quality risks

- salinity
- sodicity
- bicarbonate
- chloride
- nitrate
- biological contamination
- industrial contamination

## 10. Automated control safeguards

- manual override
- maximum runtime
- minimum off-time
- flow confirmation
- tank-level check
- dry-run protection
- rain lockout
- leak detection
- audit trail
- fail-safe state

## 11. Advisory outputs

- irrigate or wait
- target depth or volume
- expected runtime
- priority zone
- reason
- confidence
- next reassessment time
